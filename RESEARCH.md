## Executive summary

Alpha Tau Medical is an Israeli oncology **device** company developing **Alpha DaRT** — intratumoral Radium-224 seeds that release short-lived alpha-emitting daughters (~mm-scale dose cloud). The near-term US value driver is **ReSTART** (NCT05323253), a **single-arm pivotal** study in recurrent cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (cSCC) with co-primary **ORR + DOR at 6 months**. Enrollment completed at **88 patients** (May 2026); top-line expected **~end 2026**; first **modular PMA** module submitted **Jan 2026** (**verified**).

Parallel platform bets: **REGAIN** recurrent GBM feasibility (interim **n=3** only — **partial**), **IMPACT** pancreatic pilot (**n=40**, safety-primary — **verified**), **prostate pilot** (NCT07290998, **n≈12**) with **Tolmar** US commercial deal (**verified**). **Japan Shonin** approval for selected head & neck cancers (**Feb 2026**, first ex-Israel — **verified**); commercial revenue timing **unverified**.

**Bull case (verified elements):** Breakthrough Device + modular PMA path for cSCC; encouraging early GBM/pancreatic signals; Japan regulatory validation; Tolmar validates urology commercial path; ~$80M liquidity (Mar 2026); five concurrent US IDEs.

**Bear case (verified elements):** Pre-revenue, widening losses, **F-3 $300M shelf + $100M ATM** (Apr 2026); single-arm PMA vs historical controls; REGAIN n=3 non-generalizable; Japan PMS + reimbursement lag; manufacturing scale-up; warrant remeasurement volatility; Israel HQ geopolitical chatter (**partial**).

## Trial-by-trial diligence

### ReSTART (NCT05323253) — pivotal cSCC

| Item | Detail | Tag |
|------|--------|-----|
| Population | Recurrent cSCC failed ≥1L SoC; no surgery/RT; no curative systemic; **no nodal/distant mets** | verified — CT.gov |
| Co-primary endpoints | ORR (confirmed BOR, RECIST 1.1) + **DOR at 6 months** | verified — CT.gov |
| Enrollment | **88** (company PR); CT.gov est. 86 | verified |
| Sites | 28 sites US/Israel/Canada | verified — CT.gov |
| Secondary endpoints | PFS + OS at 1 yr; overall DOR; local control; QoL | verified — [SEC 6-K May 2026](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1871321/000121390026053589/ea028978301ex99-1.htm) |
| PMA | Modular PMA module 1 **Jan 2026**; full submission **toward year-end 2026** per shareholder letter | verified |
| Top-line timing | **~end 2026** | verified — forward-looking |
| Historical ORR bench | Systemic therapy ~26–40%; cemiplimab mCSCC **47%** / laCSCC **49%** (FDA); pembrolizumab r/m **~34%** / LA **50%** (KEYNOTE-629) | verified — [FDA cemiplimab](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-approvals-and-databases/fda-approves-cemiplimab-rwlc-metastatic-or-locally-advanced-cutaneous-squamous-cell-carcinoma) · PubMed |

**Community claims:**
- "PMA any day now" → **rejected** (data maturation + modular submission timeline)
- "ORR will match FIH 78.6% CR" → **rejected** (FIH n=28 lesions, different design — [31759075](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31759075/))
- "Enrollment done = approval imminent" → **partial** (enrollment verified; approval requires ORR/DOR + PMA review)

**Regulatory bear point:** Single-arm vs historical control — FDA device guidance notes observational bias in non-randomized designs ([FDA device design guidance](https://fda.report/media/87225/FDA-Guidance--Design-Considerations-for-Pivotal-Clinical-Investigations-for-Medical-Devices.pdf)). Breakthrough + modular PMA mitigates but does not eliminate risk — **partial**.
